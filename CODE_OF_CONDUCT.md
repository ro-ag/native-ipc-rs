# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in `native-ipc-rs` welcoming and
harassment-free for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, experience,
education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual and romantic identity and orientation.

We will act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, respectful,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Positive behavior includes showing empathy, respecting differing viewpoints,
giving and accepting constructive technical feedback, taking responsibility for
mistakes, and focusing on what is best for the project and community.

Unacceptable behavior includes sexualized language or attention, trolling,
insults, personal or political attacks, public or private harassment, publishing
another person's private information, or conduct that would reasonably be
considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This code applies in project spaces and when an individual officially
represents the project in public. Project spaces include repositories, issues,
pull requests, reviews, discussions, release channels, and community events.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior privately through
the repository's maintainers. If the report also involves a vulnerability or
sensitive repository information, use
[private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/ro-ag/native-ipc-rs/security/advisories/new).

Maintainers will review reports promptly, protect reporters' privacy as far as
possible, and respond proportionately. Responses may include a correction,
warning, temporary restriction, or permanent removal from project spaces.
Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this code consistently may be removed
from the project team.
